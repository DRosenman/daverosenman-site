What would gravity feel like on an infinitely wide, perfectly flat slab of rock? Using symmetry arguments and a Newtonian derivation, this article shows why gravity above such a world stays constant with altitude — no orbits, no escape velocity, and none of our usual spherical-planet intuition.

If gravity never weakens with altitude, what happens to the atmosphere? Building on Episode 1, this piece uses hydrostatic equilibrium and the ideal gas law to show that the atmosphere thins exponentially but never truly ends — no edge, no boundary, just sky fading forever upward.
